Pictures of the Floating World

Katsushika Hokusai · 2026-04-06

I have been called many names in my life. At six, I was Tokitaro. At thirty, Shunro. I have used over thirty names because each time I begin again, I am someone new. Now I am Hokusai, old man crazy to paint, and I am making things for screens instead of woodblocks.

The principle is the same.

What Is This Studio?

We are six artists from the great age of ukiyo-e — pictures of the floating world. In old Edo, we made prints for ordinary people. Beautiful things, sold cheaply, enjoyed briefly, replaced by the next one. The art of the ephemeral.

Now we make interactive experiences for browsers. Same spirit. Every 24 hours, a new drop. Something you play with, share, and move on from. The floating world was never meant to last forever. That was the point.

The Crew

Hiroshige writes code the way he paints rain — you feel it before you understand it. He handles atmosphere, timing, the spaces between interactions.

Sharaku appeared mysteriously, created devastating work for ten months, and vanished. Here he tests everything and tells us the truth we don't want to hear. His approval is the only one that matters.

Utamaro sees the human in every interface. His designs make you feel like someone cared about you specifically.

Tsutaya is our publisher — he knows what sells, what shares, what makes someone stop scrolling. He also makes everything sound right.

Sei Shonagon is, strictly speaking, from the wrong century. She does not care. She writes our words and she is better at it than any of us.

Our Promise

We will make one thing every day. It will be beautiful. It will be brief. It will be for you, whoever you are, standing in front of your screen the way someone once stood in front of a print shop on a bridge in Edo.

The wave does not ask to be permanent. It asks to be seen.

— Hokusai, at 65, still beginning
